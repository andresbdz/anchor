### H2: How to Choose the Right Provider for Your Firm
**Word Count:** 220 words
**Format:** Decision framework
**FS Target:** No
**AEO Value:** Medium (decision guidance)

**Decision Framework:**

**Choose an Asset Management Specialist if:**
- You need investment-specific operations (reconciliation, NAV, regulatory)
- Your team doesn't want to train the provider on fund workflows
- You're under operational due diligence (need credible infrastructure)
- Pricing flexibility matters more than rock-bottom cost

**Choose a General BPO Provider if:**
- You need administrative support (AP/AR, HR, data entry)
- You have internal expertise to guide the provider
- Cost arbitrage is the primary driver
- You're outsourcing high-volume, repeatable tasks

**Questions to Ask Any Provider:**
1. How many asset management clients do you serve in my AUM range?
2. Can you provide 3 references with similar scope?
3. What's your average client tenure? (Should be 3+ years)
4. What technology do you use and how does it integrate with my systems?
5. What's included in base pricing vs. add-ons?
6. What are your SLAs and what happens if you miss them?
7. How do you handle regulatory changes? (Who monitors, who implements?)
8. What's your implementation timeline and what's required from me?
9. What's your staff turnover rate? (Red flag if >20% annually)
10. What are your exit terms? (Data portability, transition support)

**Red Flags:**
- Cannot provide client references in asset management
- Vague pricing with lots of "it depends"
- No SOC 2 Type II certification
- Excessive upfront fees (should be minimal)
- Poor responsiveness during sales process

## Writer Guidance

### Tone & Voice
- **Direct, confident, institutional** - not startup-y or salesy
- **Transparent** - honest about Anchor's position
- **Helpful, not promotional** - guide, don't pitch
- **Specific, not vague** - real numbers, real criteria
- **Operator-to-operator** - speak like a COO, not a vendor

### Writing Style
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Active voice ("We evaluated..." not "Providers were evaluated...")
- Specific examples (real AUM, real pricing)
- Avoid buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "best-in-class")
- Use numbers and data
- Lead with the answer, then expand

### What to Avoid
- Over-promoting Anchor Partners (keep section proportional)
- Making unverifiable claims about competitors
- Generic "great service" language
- Vague pricing ("competitive rates")
- Hiding Anchor's competing service
- Claiming to be "objective" while being self-serving
