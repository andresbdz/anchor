### H2: Back Office Outsourcing Pricing Models
**Word Count:** 350 words
**Format:** Pricing breakdown + comparison box + examples
**Featured Snippet Target:** Yes (pricing range)
**Schema:** N/A

**Content:**

**Intro (1-2 sentences):**
"Back office outsourcing pricing varies by scope, complexity, and AUM. Here are the four common pricing models and when each makes sense."

**1. AUM-Based Pricing (Most Common)**
- Structure: X basis points of assets under management
- Typical range: 2-8 bps depending on services included
- Example: $200M AUM at 5 bps = $100,000/year
- Best for: Firms with growing AUM (cost scales naturally)
- Watch for: Minimum fees (often $3,000-$5,000/month regardless of AUM)

**2. Fixed Monthly Retainer**
- Structure: Flat monthly fee for defined scope
- Typical range: $3,000-$15,000/month for emerging managers
- Example: $5,000/month for trade rec + investor reporting = $60,000/year
- Best for: Small firms with stable workflows
- Watch for: Scope creep charges

**3. Per-Transaction Pricing**
- Structure: Fee per trade, per report, per statement
- Typical range: $5-$50 per transaction depending on complexity
- Example: 200 trades/month at $10/trade = $24,000/year
- Best for: Low-volume managers (< 100 trades/month)
- Watch for: Costs spike as volume grows

**4. Hybrid Pricing**
- Structure: Base fee + variable component (transactions or AUM)
- Example: $3,000/month base + 2 bps AUM = $48,000-$60,000/year for $200M fund
- Best for: Firms wanting cost predictability with scalability
- Watch for: Complexity in forecasting total cost

**Cost comparison box (highlighted):**

"For a $200M AUM hedge fund with 300 trades/month:
- In-house back office: $200,000-$300,000/year (2-3 FTEs loaded)
- Outsourced (AUM-based): $80,000-$120,000/year (4-6 bps)
- Annual savings: $100,000-$180,000
- ROI: 50-60% cost reduction"

**What's typically included:**
- Reconciliation, accounting, reporting, filings (core services)

**What's typically NOT included (additional fees):**
- System licenses (Advent, Bloomberg, Eze) - often pass-through
- Setup/onboarding fees: $5,000-$25,000 one-time
- Major project work (system migration, audit support)

"Back office handles post-trade operations (settlement, reconciliation, accounting, reporting). Middle office handles pre-settlement functions (risk management, compliance monitoring, trade support). ## Content Writing Guidelines

### Tone and Voice
- **Professional but approachable:** Write like a trusted operations consultant, not a salesperson
- **Direct and specific:** No vague claims. Use numbers, examples, timelines.
- **Confident:** "Back office outsourcing saves 30-50%" not "may potentially reduce costs"
- **No buzzwords:** Avoid "synergy," "leverage," "robust," "cutting-edge" unless genuinely descriptive
- **No hype:** Don't oversell. Honest assessment of pros and cons builds trust.

### Writing Style
- Lead with the most important information (inverted pyramid)
- Use short sentences (15-20 words average)
- One idea per paragraph
- Use transition words: "However," "Additionally," "For example," "In contrast"
- Prefer active voice: "Providers manage reconciliation" not "Reconciliation is managed by providers"
- Avoid passive constructions when possible

### Specific to Asset Management Audience
- **Assume knowledge of basic terms:** Don't over-explain NAV, AUM, basis points
- **Define technical outsourcing terms:** SOC 2, SLA, parallel run
- **Speak to pain points:** "20 hours/week on reconciliation," "missed investor reporting deadlines"
- **Quantify ROI:** Always express savings in both dollars and basis points
- **Address regulatory concerns:** SEC, DOL, operational due diligence

### Red Flags to Avoid (AI Content Markers)
- Generic openings: "In today's fast-paced business environment..."
- Vague claims: "significantly reduce costs," "improve efficiency"
- No specifics: "many firms," "industry experts say"
- Repetitive phrasing across sections
- No unique perspective or insight
- Lack of real-world examples
- Overly formal or robotic tone

### Example of Good vs. Bad Writing

**BAD (Generic AI Content):**
"Back office outsourcing can help businesses improve operational efficiency while reducing costs. Many companies are turning to outsourcing providers to handle these critical functions. This allows organizations to focus on their core competencies and strategic initiatives."

**GOOD (Specific, Expert):**
"For a $200M hedge fund, in-house back office operations cost $200,000-$300,000/year (2-3 FTEs). Outsourcing the same functions costs $80,000-$120,000/year (4-6 bps), saving $100,000+ annually. That's equivalent to reclaiming 20+ hours per week that portfolio managers can redirect to research and alpha generation."
