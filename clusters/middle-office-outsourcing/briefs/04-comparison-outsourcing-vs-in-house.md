## AI-Citation Ready Definition

**Place in first 60 words:**

> Choosing between middle office outsourcing and in-house operations depends on firm size, complexity, and growth plans. Outsourcing offers lower fixed costs and specialized expertise; in-house provides more control and customization. Most firms under $500M AUM benefit from outsourcing, while larger firms often use hybrid models.

### H2: When Outsourcing Makes Sense (250 words)

#### H3: Firm Size Under $500M AUM
- Economics don't support dedicated team
- Variable cost model fits growth stage
- Access to institutional-grade processes

#### H3: Rapid Growth Phase
- Operations need to scale faster than hiring
- Avoid building then rebuilding

#### H3: Complex Multi-Asset Strategies
- Specialized expertise required
- Not your core competency

#### H3: Investor ODD Requirements
- Institutional expectations exceed current capabilities
- Faster path to compliance

### H2: When In-House Makes Sense (250 words)

#### H3: Scale Over $1B+ AUM
- Economics favor dedicated team
- Customization needs are high

#### H3: Proprietary Processes
- Competitive advantage in operations
- Unique data requirements

#### H3: Regulatory Sensitivity
- Direct control required
- In-house expertise already exists

#### H3: Strong Existing Team
- Already have the talent
- Cultural preference for control
