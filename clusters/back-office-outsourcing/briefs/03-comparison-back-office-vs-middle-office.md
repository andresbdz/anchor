#### H2: What is the Front Office? (180-200 words)

**Purpose:** Clear definition + establish context

**Content Points:**
- **Definition (first sentence):** "The front office encompasses all revenue-generating, client-facing functions in an asset management firm."
- **Key Functions:**
  - Portfolio management and investment decisions
  - Trading and execution
  - Sales and client relationships
  - Investment research and analysis
  - Business development and fundraising
- **Why It Matters:** Front office is the firm's competitive differentiator
- **Outsourcing Reality:** Rarely outsourced because it represents core competency
- **Asset Management Context:** This is where investment expertise creates value

**Keyword Integration:** Use "front office" and "front office functions" naturally (2-3 times).

#### H2: What is the Middle Office? (240-260 words)

**Purpose:** Define + explain the "bridge" concept + establish outsourcing relevance

**Content Points:**
- **Definition (first sentence):** "The middle office handles risk management, compliance, and trade support—serving as the bridge between trading (front office) and settlement (back office)."
- **Key Functions:**
  - Risk analytics and monitoring
  - Compliance and regulatory oversight
  - Performance measurement and attribution
  - Trade support and confirmation
  - P&L reconciliation
  - Investment book of record (IBOR) management
- **The Bridge Concept:** Middle office translates front office decisions into actionable data for back office processing
- **Growing Complexity:** Regulatory requirements have expanded middle office scope significantly
- **Outsourcing Trend:** Increasingly outsourced due to specialized expertise requirements and technology costs
- **Asset Management Context:** Critical for firms managing multiple strategies or complex instruments

**Keyword Integration:** Use "middle office functions" and "what is middle office" naturally (2-3 times).

**Quotable Fact:** "Middle office outsourcing is growing at 10.6% CAGR, driven by regulatory complexity and technology costs."

#### H2: What is the Back Office? (240-260 words)

**Purpose:** Define + establish as most outsource-ready + connect to Anchor's services

**Content Points:**
- **Definition (first sentence):** "The back office manages all post-trade operational and administrative functions that ensure accurate settlement, accounting, and reporting."
- **Key Functions:**
  - Trade settlement and confirmation
  - Cash and position reconciliation
  - Fund accounting and NAV calculation
  - Investor reporting and statements
  - Regulatory filings (Form PF, Form ADV, etc.)
  - Corporate actions processing
  - Transfer agency coordination
- **The Operational Backbone:** Back office ensures accuracy and compliance in everything that happens after a trade
- **Rules-Based Nature:** Most back office functions follow defined processes, making them ideal for automation and outsourcing
- **Outsourcing Reality:** Most commonly outsourced office type due to:
  - Commoditized processes
  - Economies of scale
  - Clear SLAs and measurable outcomes
- **Asset Management Context:** For firms under $500M AUM, in-house back office often represents 20-30% of headcount

**Keyword Integration:** Use "back office functions" and "what is back office" naturally (2-3 times).

**Quotable Fact:** "[X]% of asset managers under $500M AUM outsource at least one back office function."

#### H2: Key Differences Explained (240-260 words)

**Purpose:** Deepen understanding through specific comparison points

**Content Structure:** Use H3 subheadings for each comparison dimension

##### H3: Revenue vs. Support
- **Front Office:** Directly generates returns through investment decisions
- **Middle Office:** Protects revenue by managing risk and ensuring compliance
- **Back Office:** Enables operations but doesn't directly impact performance

##### H3: Timing in the Trade Lifecycle
- **Front Office:** Pre-trade (research, decisions, execution)
- **Middle Office:** Trade + post-trade (confirmation, risk checks, compliance)
- **Back Office:** Post-trade (settlement, booking, reconciliation)

**Visual Suggestion:** Simple trade lifecycle diagram or text-based flowchart

##### H3: Skill Sets Required
- **Front Office:** Investment acumen, market knowledge, client relationship skills
- **Middle Office:** Analytical skills, regulatory knowledge, systems expertise
- **Back Office:** Process-oriented, detail-focused, accounting knowledge

##### H3: Technology Requirements
- **Front Office:** Order management systems (OMS), research platforms, trading tools
- **Middle Office:** Risk management systems, compliance software, IBOR platforms
- **Back Office:** Portfolio accounting systems, reconciliation tools, reporting platforms

**Keyword Integration:** Use "back office front office difference" naturally (1 time).

## Notes for Writer

### Voice and Tone
- Direct and confident, not salesy
- Speak like an experienced operator, not a consultant
- Use "you" to address the reader (asset manager COO/CFO)
- Avoid jargon where possible; define it when necessary
- No buzzwords or empty claims

### Asset Management Context
- All examples should reference asset managers, not banks or trading firms
- Reference common firm sizes ($50M-$500M AUM sweet spot)
- Mention specific workflows: NAV calculation, trade reconciliation, investor reporting
- Acknowledge different firm types: RIAs, hedge funds, private equity

### Outsourcing Positioning
- Don't be aggressive about selling Anchor's services
- Keep focus on education and helping reader understand distinctions
- Outsourcing should be presented as one option, not the only option
- ROI and time savings should be specific and believable

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Don't say "seamless" or "cutting-edge"
- Don't promise "elimination" of back office work (we take it on, not eliminate it)
- Don't over-complicate with trading jargon
- Don't make the comparison about technology vendors
