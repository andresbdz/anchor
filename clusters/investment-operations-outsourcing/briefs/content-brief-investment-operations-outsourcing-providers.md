#### **1. Northern Trust Investment Operations Outsourcing (150-180 words)**
**Headline (H3):** Northern Trust — Global Custodian with Full-Service Operations

**What to Cover:**
- **Overview:** Global custodian with investment operations as part of integrated custody + admin offering
- **Best For:** Large asset managers ($1B+ AUM) needing bundled custody, fund admin, and ops
- **Cost Structure:** Typically AUM-based pricing (30-50 bps for full-service depending on complexity). Minimum engagement ~$500M AUM.
- **Specialization:** Broad multi-asset coverage. Strong in traditional long-only, PE, real assets. Less specialized in niche credit strategies.
- **Technology:** Proprietary platform (Integrated Trading Solutions). Strong reporting but limited API flexibility for smaller clients.
- **Pros:** Brand credibility, regulatory compliance infrastructure, institutional client references.
- **Cons:** Pricing premium, slow onboarding (6-12 months typical), less flexible for sub-$1B managers.

**Keyword Integration:** "northern trust investment operations outsourcing" naturally in subheading and body.

**Internal Link Opportunity:** Link to pillar page on "What is Investment Operations Outsourcing" for context.

#### **2. SEI Investment Operations Services (150-180 words)**
**Headline (H3):** SEI — Middle-Market Focus with Proprietary Tech

**What to Cover:**
- **Overview:** Mid-tier provider targeting $250M-$5B AUM managers. Combines fund admin with operations.
- **Best For:** Traditional asset managers (mutual funds, SMAs) looking for bundled middle/back office.
- **Cost Structure:** Tiered AUM pricing (25-40 bps). Minimum ~$250M AUM.
- **Specialization:** Strong in retail-facing strategies (mutual funds, ETFs). Less depth in private markets or credit.
- **Technology:** SEI Wealth Platform (SWP) — proprietary, robust for traditional strategies but limited customization.
- **Pros:** Predictable pricing, established workflows for regulated products, good client service reputation.
- **Cons:** Not designed for private credit or CLO workflows. Platform flexibility limited.

**E-E-A-T Signal:** Cite client count or AUA statistics if publicly available (e.g., "SEI reports $1.4T in AUA across investment operations clients as of Q4 2025").

### **How to Choose the Right Provider for Your Firm (200-250 words)**
**Headline (H2):** Choosing the Right Investment Operations Partner

**Purpose:** Help reader self-segment and identify 1-2 providers to contact.

**Decision Framework:**

**If your firm has:**
- **$1B+ AUM, multiple strategies, global presence** → Northern Trust, IQ-EQ
- **$250M-$1B AUM, traditional long-only or liquid alts** → SEI
- **$100M+ AUM, private equity or venture capital focus** → STP, Maples (if offshore)
- **$200M+ AUM, multi-strategy with international needs** → IQ-EQ
- **$50M-$500M AUM, credit-focused strategy** → Anchor Partners, STP

**Questions to Ask Before Contracting:**
1. What is your typical client AUM range? (Avoid being under-served or over-paying)
2. Do you have clients in my specific asset class? (Ask for references)
3. What is your fee structure and minimum engagement? (Get pricing transparency early)
4. How long is typical onboarding? (Understand time-to-value)
5. Can I add services incrementally or is it bundled only? (Assess lock-in risk)

**E-E-A-T Signal:** "These questions are based on common vendor selection errors documented in the 2025 Greenwich Associates study on asset manager outsourcing."
